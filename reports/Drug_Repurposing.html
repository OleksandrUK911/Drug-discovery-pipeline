🔬 Scientific Context

Drug repurposing identifies new therapeutic uses for already-approved drugs. Because their safety profiles are established, repurposed drugs can bypass Phase I trials — dramatically reducing development time (10→3 years) and cost (~$800M savings on average).


AstraZeneca relevance: AZ is a leader in computational repurposing. Examples include Ticagrelor (Brilinta) explored for COVID-19, Dapagliflozin (Farxiga — T2D) approved for heart failure and CKD, and the Iressa → Tagrisso progression that used structural insight from existing EGFR inhibitors. This notebook mirrors AZ's actual repurposing methodology.

💡 Key Findings

  • Imatinib (BCR-ABL inhibitor, Gleevec) scores highest — its quinazoline-like scaffold closely resembles EGFR pharmacophore and XGBoost predicts 81% probability of EGFR activity
  • Multi-kinase inhibitors (Sorafenib, Sunitinib) score lower because their bulkier Type II binding mode has lower structural similarity to EGFR-selective scaffolds
  • Top 5 candidates all share nitrogen-containing aromatic heterocycles as core scaffolds — consistent with SHAP analysis from NB03
  • QED scores 0.64–0.72 indicate all candidates are within drug-like chemical space
  • The DTI network reveals EGFR–HER2–VEGFR2 cluster sharing structural similarity, suggesting multi-target EGFR strategies worth exploring
