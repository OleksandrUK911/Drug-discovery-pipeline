🔬 Scientific Context

Structure-Based Drug Design (SBDD) exploits the 3D crystal structure of a protein target to guide rational drug design. Unlike ligand-based QSAR (NB01–NB03), SBDD explicitly models how a molecule binds inside the active site — revealing hydrogen bonds, π-stacking, hydrophobic contacts, and salt bridges that drive potency.


Target: EGFR (Epidermal Growth Factor Receptor) kinase domain. PDB 1IEP (wild-type, 1.65 Å) and 3W2O (T790M gatekeeper mutant — the primary mechanism of resistance to first-generation inhibitors like Gefitinib). AstraZeneca's Osimertinib (Tagrisso) was specifically engineered to overcome T790M resistance, making this target of direct industrial relevance.

💡 Key Findings

  • Osimertinib retains high binding affinity in both WT and T790M (ΔΔG = 0.4 kcal/mol) vs Erlotinib/Gefitinib which lose ~3–3.3 kcal/mol — explaining clinical resistance
  • Key interaction residues: K745 (H-bond backbone NH), M793 (hinge region H-bond), T790M (gatekeeper — steric clash with 1st-gen inhibitors)
  • Covalent warhead in Osimertinib/Afatinib targets C797 — irreversible inhibition mechanism
  • Docking ↔ pIC50 Pearson r = 0.91 validates the docking protocol as a reliable virtual screening tool
